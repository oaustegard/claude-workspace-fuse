## 2026-03-17 — analysis (p1) `8bbf4699`
_tags: research, brain-drain, student-visas, us-china, STEM, education, geopolitics_

RESEARCH: Chinese student visas to US and brain drain dynamics, 2015-2026.

ENROLLMENT DATA:
- Peak Chinese enrollment in US: 372,532 in 2019-20
- 2014-15: ~304,000; steady growth through 2019
- 2020-21: sharp COVID drop, but diverged from other countries' recovery
- 2021-22: 290,086 (down 21% from peak while non-Chinese enrollment grew 10.3%)
- 2022-23: 289,526 (lowest since 2013-14)
- 2023-24: ~277,000 (4% year-over-year decrease per Open Doors)
- 2024-25: 265,919 (continuing decline)
- India overtook China as #1 source country

KEY POLICY INFLECTION POINTS:
1. 2018: DOJ launches "China Initiative" - FBI agents on campuses, investigations of Chinese-descent researchers. ~90% of defendants were of Chinese heritage. <30% conviction rate vs >90% federal average. Biden ended it Feb 2022.
2. 2020: Trump Proclamation 10043 - restricted visas for Chinese grad students from "military-civil" universities. ~1,000 visas revoked. Biden maintained this.
3. 2020 summer: Only 516 student visas issued to Chinese students (vs 64,027 same period prior year) - 99% drop
4. May 2025: Rubio announces "aggressive" revocation of Chinese student visas for those with CCP connections or in critical fields
5. 2025-26: Continued escalation - social media screening, paused visa interviews at embassies

BRAIN DRAIN / STAY RATE DATA:
- As of 2017, ~90% of Chinese STEM PhDs (2000-2015 cohorts) still in US
- 87% of China-born non-citizen new PhDs (2005-2015) intended to stay
- BUT: After China Initiative (2018), departures of Chinese-descent scientists increased 75%
- ~20,000 Chinese-born scientists left US between 2010-2021, accelerating after 2018
- Two-thirds of relocated scientists moved to China specifically
- 61% of surveyed Chinese-descent scientists considered leaving US
- 45% avoiding federal grant applications
- Stay rate declines 2.1 percentage points per year of green card delay

CHINA'S SIDE:
- Thousand Talents Plan (2008+) actively recruiting returnees
- By 2020, claimed 80% of Chinese STEM PhDs abroad returning (up from 5% historically)
- Chinese university enrollment exceeded 37 million in 2023, 10+ million graduates
- China surpassed US in total publication volume ~2017-2020
- China overtook US in Nature Index (elite journals) in 2022-2024
- 7 of top 10 institutions by research volume are Chinese (2024-25)
- China produces 40% more citable papers than US
- China STEM PhD growth rate: ~9% annually vs US ~3%
- By 2020: China awarding ~2 million STEM bachelor's vs US ~900,000
- 3.6 million patents in force (2022) vs US 3.3 million

OSKAR'S HYPOTHESIS: Making student visas harder = boost to China, loss to US by slowing brain drain.
Evidence largely supports this, with important nuances about quality vs quantity and counterfactuals.
